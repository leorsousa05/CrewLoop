# Typography Playbook

Typography is the identity layer.

## Rules

- Use at most three families: display, body, and optional mono.
- The display face should have character. It should not disappear into the background.
- The body face must be highly readable for long-form scanning.
- Mono should be reserved for code, paths, labels, and operational metadata.

## Type strategies

### Editorial
- Display serif with personality.
- Clean body face with measured line length.
- Use size and spacing to create rhythm.

### Industrial
- Compressed or semi-condensed display face.
- Clear monospace utility layer.
- Use weight contrast rather than decorative styling.

### Luxury
- Elegant display face.
- Quiet body face.
- Wider leading and careful letter spacing.

## Common failures

- Default system sans as the brand face.
- Too many weights with no hierarchy.
- Overly tight leading on long paragraphs.
- Decorative headings that hurt legibility.

## Practical hierarchy

- Hero: strong display size with tight but not cramped line-height.
- Section title: distinct but less dramatic than hero.
- Body: comfortable reading measure.
- Meta: smaller, muted, but still legible.
