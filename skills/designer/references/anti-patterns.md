# Anti-Patterns

These are the failure modes that usually produce generic AI output.

## Visual failures to avoid

- Purple-to-blue gradient hero on an off-white background with no brand reason.
- Three-column feature grid with identical cards and equal weight on every item.
- Glassmorphism used as decoration instead of depth or layering.
- Default `Inter` / `Roboto` / `Arial` type stacks as the main identity.
- Floating neon cards that ignore content hierarchy.
- Centered hero + centered CTA + centered everything else.
- Generic SaaS palette with no dominant color family.
- Decorative mesh gradients that interfere with legibility.
- Overly rounded cards that all look interchangeable.
- A page that could belong to any product if the logo were removed.

## Why they fail

- They erase product identity.
- They hide hierarchy instead of clarifying it.
- They read as prompt output rather than authored design.
- They do not tell the user what the product feels like to use.

## Correction cues

- Replace generic gradients with a context-specific palette.
- Replace symmetric grids with asymmetrical composition.
- Replace type neutrality with a face that has character.
- Replace decoration-first motion with meaning-first motion.
- Replace card sameness with varied hierarchy and density.
