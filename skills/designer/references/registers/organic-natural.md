# Register: Organic / Natural

## When to use
- Wellness, food, sustainability, skincare, agriculture, lifestyle, and slow-living brands.
- Marketing pages, brand sites, and product stories where trust comes from warmth and material honesty.
- Content surfaces led by real photography of ingredients, people, landscapes, or process.

## When NOT to use
- Dashboards, admin tools, forms, settings, or any productivity surface — those stay Quiet.
- Fintech, dev tools, or B2B SaaS where earthy tones read as unserious or vague.
- Data-dense screens: soft palettes and photography compete with information density.
- Any brief that never mentions nature, craft, food, or wellbeing — do not force it.

## Palette philosophy
- Ground in warm neutrals: backgrounds `hsl(35-45, 20-35%, 94-97%)` (cream, oat, paper).
- Secondary earth tones: terracotta `hsl(15-25, 45-60%, 45-60%)`, clay, sand, mushroom.
- One green family as the accent: `hsl(90-150, 25-45%, 25-40%)` — moss, olive, or forest, never neon or mint.
- Text is deep brown-black `hsl(25-35, 25-35%, 12-18%)`, not pure `#000`.
- Contrast posture: body text ≥4.5:1 on cream backgrounds; keep accent buttons dark enough to pass on light surfaces — test the green, it is the usual failure point.
- Forbidden here: pure grays (`hsl(0, 0%, *)`), pure white backgrounds, saturated primaries, purple-to-blue gradients.

## Typography
- Pair one serif with one sans: serif for display and headlines (a warm oldstyle or transitional serif), humanist sans for body and UI.
- Display sizes are generous but not shouty: H1 40-64px, weight 400-500, line-height 1.1-1.2 — the serif carries the voice, not the weight.
- Body 16-18px, line-height 1.6-1.75 — this register sells calm, so let text breathe.
- Labels and buttons in the sans, 13-15px, weight 500-600, normal or slightly wide letter-spacing (+0.01em).
- Never use condensed grotesques, mono fonts, or all-caps display — they fight the warmth.

## Textures & effects
- Allowed: real photography (full-bleed or soft-cropped), subtle paper grain at 2-5% opacity, thin hand-drawn-style divider lines, generous whitespace as the primary "texture".
- Radii are soft but not toy-like: 8-16px on cards, 999px on pills and buttons is acceptable on marketing CTAs.
- Shadows: nearly none. At most a single diffuse shadow `0 2px 12px hsl(30 30% 20% / 0.06)` on floating cards.
- Forbidden: glassmorphism, neon glows, mesh gradients, heavy blur, drop-shadow stacks, glossy or skeuomorphic effects, stock photos of handshakes and skylines.

## Motion flavor
- Stay inside the skill's motion diet: 0-3 small animations, each justified. Calm is the brand — motion must not interrupt it.
- Feedback transitions: 150-250ms, `ease-out`, `transform`/`opacity` only.
- The one expressive allowance: a slow image crossfade (300-500ms, opacity only) in hero or gallery swaps, because photography is the content.
- Off-limits: spring/bounce overshoot, scroll-jacking, parallax, cursor effects, looping ambient animation, animated gradients. On any tool surface, the budget drops to zero with a "no motion" declaration.
- Every animation ships a reduced-motion fallback (instant swap).

## Layout idioms
- Full-bleed photography hero with a short serif headline set on a calm area of the image or on cream beside it.
- Asymmetric editorial splits: 55/45 image-text columns that alternate sides per section.
- Large vertical rhythm: 96-160px section spacing; content column 640-720px for prose.
- Ingredient/feature cards with soft radius, photography on top, 2-3 per row maximum.
- Pull quotes or provenance callouts ("sourced from", "made with") set in the serif at 20-24px.

## Do / Don't
- Do:
  - Anchor every screen with at least one piece of real photography.
  - Derive the green accent from the actual photography, not from a palette generator.
  - Keep one serif + one sans and stop there.
  - Use cream/oat backgrounds instead of white on every surface.
  - Verify the green accent passes 4.5:1 for button text before shipping.
- Don't:
  - Don't use leaf icons, leaf dividers, or leaf watermarks on every section — one botanical motif per page, maximum.
  - Don't pair the earthy palette with pure gray UI chrome; warm the neutrals instead.
  - Don't apply this register to the product's settings or checkout forms — those go Quiet.
  - Don't use stock-photo clichés (smiling-with-salad, hands-holding-plant) without brand justification.
  - Don't add grain, shadows, and illustration all at once — pick one texture layer.
